#### Essential Info & Tips

The key to combat in RAD is to fight dirty and hit the enemy when they're down or otherwise incapacitated. This can be when they are stunned and falling down, when they've fallen and are getting up, and when they've been launched into the air. Conversely, you want to block any attacks, especially those that will knock you down or launch you into the buildings behind you. 
+ Chop, straight punch, and uppercut will guarantee knockdown if they aren't blocked
+ Hook and backhand will knock down some but all enemies if they aren't blocked
+ Enemies that are falling can be attacked again to re-start their falling animation, although only Laguiole can attack fast enough to reliably do this
+ Enemies that have fallen and are getting up cannot be launched or knocked down, which allows you to use powerful attacks that would usually launch the enemy far away from you
+ Chop deals 66% the damage of the other two but keeps the enemy closest to you, which is invaluable for combo
+ Hook and backhand take less time to execute than chop/straight/uppercut, and can re-start the fall animation if an enemy is already stunned/falling
+ Blocking with two hands will reduce damage and prevent knockdown from some attacks, but prevents movement
+ Blocking with one hands won't reduce damage but WILL prevent knockdown from some attacks, and can be used while walking
+ Energy can be recovered by attacking enemies (amount recovered scales with damage dealt), or you can recover 5en by taking a step. Energy recovered from stepping happens when the step animation starts, so in normal mode you can repeatedly tap L1 or R1 to "quickly" gain energy while keeping your distance from enemies.

<h1 id=Challenges>
    🏆 Progression and Missables 
</h1>

There are achievements for each of the three possible endings, so mastery will require at least three full playthroughs of story mode to ensure you complete all missable achievements.

### _Playthrough 1 - Ellen Ending as Naoto/Ryo with Gllang_
Curry Ellen's favor and avoid Nanao's, and aim for post-mission events. This will involve a lot of city damage, so don’t worry about awards this time. Get all Gllang's upgrades, and Pilot too if you have enough funds.
- After episode 8, destroy Nanao’s workplace in Downtown Senjo until all 5 post-mission Nanao events are unlocked
- After episode 8, destroy Keiko’s apartment in Downtown Senjo until all 3 post-mission Keiko events are unlocked
- Keep destroying Misaki HQ and at least one Misaki plant anytime you're in New Senjo City; you want Misaki's $ count to be 9+ before you hit ep26
- Put out fires at city hall in ep14
- Do not go to/help passed-out Nanao in ep16
- Do NOT talk to Nanao twice in ep 20
- Do NOT get to school on time in ep 20
- Do NOT talk to Ellen until she gets mad in ep22
- Pick up the bus in ep 23
- Do NOT destroy Otawara or supermarket in ep31/32
- Destroy Misaki HQ in in at least three of ep39, 40, 44, 47, 48, and 51 to get the remaining Misaki post-mission events

<h1 id=Challenges>
    🏆 Challenges <i><sub><sup>(42 achievements - 210 points)</sup></sub></i>
</h1>

Most challenge achievements require completing missions while minimizing damage to the city, and can be completed in story or challenge mode. In missions where the enemy targets specific buildings and not the meganite (Controlling the Battlefield, Tower Defense) use weak ranged attacks to force the volgara to phantom away from the building.

### Damage limits
+ Downtown Senjo: 190 dmg or less for the Construction Minister Award
+  Senjo City Center: 190 dmg or less for the Construction Minister Award
+ Senjo City Center 50 Billion yen missions: For ep 19b, 39, 47, 48 you only need to stay under 450 dmg
+ Senjo Port: 50 dmg or less for the Senjo Peace Award
+ Mt Chihaya: 60 dmg or less for the Senjo Peace Award

<h4 id=RA_Measure> • Measured Indicator </h4>

An indicator displayed by the Retro Achievements overlay, often used to track specific progress within an achievement.
> **Examples**: 
> - *Headshots* - 7/12 
> - *Enemies left* - 11/100 
> - *Accuracy* - 47% 
> - *Coins collected* - 88%

<h4 id=RA_Trigger> • Triggered Indicator </h4>

An indicator displayed by the Retro Achievements overlay, typically used to show when a challenge achievement is available for unlocking or when it is currently active. If this indicator disappears before the achievement unlocks, it often signals a failure, indicating that a retry is necessary.
> **Examples**: 
> - Enough headshots acquired, finish the mission 
> - All enemies killed, kill the boss
> - Player has not yet taken a hit - for a hitless achievement
> - All requirements are met, continue the game to unlock...
